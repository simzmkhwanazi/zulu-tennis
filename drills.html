Practice — train decisions, not reps

Practice, by design

Most improvement comes from open, decision-first practice — drills where you perceive, decide, then execute, instead of grooving one predictable feed. Choose partner drills, or train hard on your own with a ball machine, a wall, or a basket of serves. The planner lets you tick the drills you ran and share a session summary.

Why "open" drills? Modern high-performance coaching (constraints-led / ecological) builds match performance by making you read and choose under realistic uncertainty — not repeat a basket feed. Start here:

🤝 With a partner

🎲 Open / decision drills

Random Feed Open

Your partner feeds to unpredictable spots — you read & react. No set pattern, so you train perception and footwork, not memory.

  • Feeder mixes side, depth & spin randomly
  • Recover to centre after every ball
  • Progress: feeder disguises the cue until late
Random-feed reaction drillpdhsports

Approach Decision — DTL vs CC Open

On a short ball you choose down-the-line vs cross-court from the ball height and the open court — the decision is the rep.

  • Feeder varies the short ball
  • Read it late, commit, recover
  • Score +1 when you pick the open court
Cross-court vs down-the-line decisionTop Tennis Training

King of the Court Open

Live-ball game: win points to hold or take the "king" end. A real scoreboard forces real decisions under pressure.

  • King feeds; challenger must win 2 to swap ends
  • Loser rotates out (3+ players) or replay (2)
  • Play singles lines
King of the Court for singlesHawaii Tennis Pro

Live-Ball / Pressure Point Open

Every point starts from a feed and is played out — often with a constraint (must approach, first to 7) to bias the pattern you're working on.

  • Feed a neutral ball to start
  • Play the point fully
  • Add one rule to shape the situation
Pressure point — live-ball drillDaily Tennis Lesson

Serve +1 (live) Open

Serve for real, read the actual return, play the +1 — the most common decision in your own service games.

  • Pick a serve target before each ball
  • React to the real return, not a script
  • Track your +1 win rate
Serve +1 live drillEssential Tennis

Situational Point Play Open

Start points from a scenario — 0-30 down, a short sitter, pulled wide — and play them out so the decision is match-real.

  • Name the situation first
  • Feed into it, then play to the end
  • Swap roles each set of reps
Practice point playThe Tennis Dream

Constraints-Led Game Open

Set a constraint — no down-the-line, must come in, hit a target zone — so the correct decision emerges naturally. No lecturing needed.

  • Pick one constraint
  • Play games inside it
  • Remove it and keep the habit

🎯 Pattern drills

Cross-Court Rally Pattern

Sustain depth and shape cross-court — your safest, highest-margin rally ball.

  • Both hit only cross-court
  • Count 10 deep in a row
  • Add a height target over the net
Deeper, consistent cross-courtTennis Hacker

Down-the-Line Change Pattern

Drill when it's worth changing direction down the line — and when it isn't.

  • Rally cross-court
  • Change DTL only off a short/high ball
  • Recover immediately; track DTL errors
Shot selection — the four zonesPatrick Smith Tennis

Serve +1 Patterns Pattern

Groove the three serve+1 patterns so the first strike is automatic.

  • Wide → open court
  • Body → jam, then open court
  • T → behind them

🥅 Net drills

Net Volleys Net

Punch and place volleys using body and feet, not the arm.

  • Partner feeds volleys to you
  • Step in, contact out in front
  • Alternate FH/BH and targets
Volley with your bodyEssential Tennis

Approach + Volley Net

Approach off a short ball, split, and close the first volley.

  • Feed short
  • Approach down-the-line
  • Split-step, then volley to the open court

🎯 Training alone

No hitting partner? You can still train hard. A ball machine, a backboard and a basket of serves cover most of the game — and movement/shadow work needs nothing but the court.

🤖 Ball machine

Ball Machine — Groundstrokes Solo

Groove FH/BH depth and consistency; the machine is your tireless feeder.

  • Set depth + moderate pace
  • 20-ball sets to one target
  • Move your feet — don't reach
5 essential ball-machine drillsMeike Babel Tennis

Ball Machine — Decisions Solo

Set the machine to oscillate / random so you must choose the shot by ball quality — not memory. The closest thing to a decision drill on your own.

  • Random or 2-line oscillation
  • Pre-decide your rule (deep → cross, short → attack)
  • Recover after every ball

🧱 Wall / backboard

Wall / Backboard Rally Solo

The wall never misses — relentless reps for control, consistency and quick hands.

  • Stand back for full strokes
  • Aim above a line for net clearance
  • Count streaks; add volley-volley for reactions
Tennis wall — 10 drills for all levelsTennis with Dylan

🎯 Serve

Serve Targets (basket) Solo

A basket and a few cones — serve to spots until the placement is automatic.

  • Place targets wide / body / T
  • Serve 10 to each
  • Score hits, not just "in"
Serve target practice drillOnline Tennis Instruction

🏃 Movement & shadow (no ball)

Shadow Swings Solo

Grooved strokes with no ball — perfect the path, finish and balance anywhere.

  • Full FH/BH motions
  • Add a split-step + recovery step
  • 3 × 1 min, slow then fast
Forehand shadow-swing drillRip It Tennis

Spider Drill Solo

Five-cone court-coverage sprint — the classic tennis movement & fitness test.

  • Touch 5 spots from the centre mark
  • Back to centre each time
  • Time it; beat your best
Tennis spider drillTennis Files

Cone Agility / Speed Solo

Cone patterns for first-step quickness, change of direction and conditioning.

  • Set a ladder of cones
  • Sprint / shuffle / backpedal patterns
  • Short rest, repeat sets
