Tournament Operating System

Peaking on the day isn't luck — it's a countdown. Work backwards from match day and remove every avoidable surprise so all that's left to do is compete.

30 days out — build

  • Finalise the training block; this is your last window for heavy physical work.
  • Play practice matches vs varied styles (especially the ones you struggle with).
  • Confirm logistics: entry, draw date, travel, accommodation, surface.
  • Lock in any equipment changes now (strings, shoes) — never new gear late.

14 days out — sharpen

  • Shift from building fitness to sharpening patterns and serve/return.
  • Reduce strength volume; keep intensity, add court-specific movement.
  • Review scouting for likely opponents (Scouting DB).
  • Dial in match-day nutrition & warm-up; rehearse them in practice.

7 days out — taper

  • Taper volume; keep it crisp and confident — quality over quantity.
  • Prioritise sleep; bank rest now.
  • Pack list ready (rackets, strings, kit, food, water, tape, sunscreen).
  • Visualise your patterns and a calm, focused you (Mental).

48 hours out — prime

  • Light, sharp hit; serve targets; nothing exhausting.
  • Hydrate well; familiar meals only; no experiments.
  • Confirm match time, court, transport; plan arrival ~60–90 min early.
  • Prepare your 3-line game plan card for the likely opponent.

Match day

  • Carb-based meal 2–3 h before; hydrate early.
  • Arrive early; dynamic warm-up + the 10-minute hit routine.
  • Re-read your 3-line plan + autopilot patterns; set the aggression dial for game 1.
  • First 2 games: high first-serve %, deep returns, read their tendencies before forcing.

Between matches (same day / next round)

  • Refuel + rehydrate immediately; protein + carbs.
  • Cool-down movement; brief note on what worked (next opponent prep).
  • Get off your feet, stay warm, stay calm; don't over-spectate other matches.

Tournament recovery

  • Sleep is the priority across multi-day events.
  • Active recovery (easy movement, mobility) over total rest between match days.
  • Manage soreness; treat niggles early.

Post-tournament review

  • Chart/aggregate results (Match Analysis); update scouting.
  • Honest review: what held up under pressure, what didn't.
  • Feed the biggest gap into the Improvement Engine and the Roadmap.
  • Plan a short recovery block before the next training cycle.
⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Training hard the week before (tired legs on day 1).
  • New gear, new food, or new tactics at the event.
  • No plan for between-match refuelling and recovery in a one-day event.
✅ Success metrics
  • You arrive rested, fuelled and with a plan every match.
  • Fewer slow starts (first-2-games win rate ↑).
  • A post-tournament review exists with one clear priority.
