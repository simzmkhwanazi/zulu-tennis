Advanced Concepts

The edges most playbooks skip — the probability thinking, analytics and adaptability that separate players who "hit well" from players who consistently win.

Expected-Value tennis

Every shot choice has a distribution of outcomes. Expected Value (EV) = (chance you win the point) − (chance you lose it), over the long run. The flashy down-the-line winner might win the point 35% of the time and lose it 40% — negative EV. The boring deep crosscourt might be +EV all day. Choose the highest-EV play, not the highest-ceiling play. This is the math under the whole Decision Engine. Level A

The single most common amateur error lives in the bottom-right box: high risk, low reward.

Pattern-probability models

Track which of your patterns actually win points (in charting). If your serve+1 to the forehand wins 68% but your DTL-change pattern wins 41%, the data tells you what to lean on and what to drill. Play your measured percentages, not your feelings.

Pressure-point strategy

Big points reward the lowest-variance plan. Pre-decide your Pressure-Point pattern and use it every time the point matters. Don't invent — execute.

Tendency tracking, location analytics & heatmaps

  • Opponent-tendency tracking: log their go-to serve/shot on big points (Scouting DB).
  • Serve-location analytics: chart where your serves land per side; find the patterns you over-use (predictable) and your reliable spots.
  • Return-position analytics: note where you stand and how it correlates with return points won — adjust depth per server.
  • Court-position heatmaps: from video, mark where you contact most balls. Living 3 metres behind the baseline? That's a heatmap problem, not a stroke problem.

Momentum management

Momentum is real but reversible. When it's against you: slow the pace, change one variable, target one service hold to stop the run. When it's with you: keep your routine identical, don't coast, press the next game. See Mental Performance.

Energy management during matches

  • Spend energy in bursts on points; fully relax between points (the 80% you're not playing).
  • Use full time between points and on changeovers when tiring; fuel/hydrate on changeovers.
  • Bank energy in easy service games; spend it on return games and big points.

GrassFast, low bounce — shorten points, serve+1, slice stays low, attack the net, take time early.

The match-intelligence system

Tie it together: pre-match scouting → in-match reading (score, tendencies, momentum, conditions) → EV-based decisions → post-match charting → priorities. That feedback loop, run every match, is what "match IQ" actually is — and it's trainable.

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Chasing highlight (high-ceiling) plays instead of high-EV ones.
  • Ignoring conditions — playing your sea-level hard-court game in wind on clay.
  • Burning energy between points and having nothing left in the third set.
✅ Success metrics
  • You can name the +EV play in a given situation, not just the exciting one.
  • You adjust at least one tactic for conditions every match.
  • Third-set win % holds steady (energy managed).
