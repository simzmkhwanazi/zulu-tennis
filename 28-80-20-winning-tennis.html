The highest-return skills (do these)

Ranked by impact on match outcomes for competitive adults. Level B

1 · First-serve percentage (with location)

Why: a high first-serve % means more free/easy points and less time defending second serves. How: drill location over power; build go-to spots. Measure: 1st-serve % (60–70%) and 1st-serve points won. Impact: ★★★★★

2 · Return depth

Why: deep returns take away the server's +1 and turn their hold into a real rally. How: block-to-deep-target drills. Measure: return-in-play % & depth. Impact: ★★★★★

3 · Crosscourt consistency & rally tolerance

Why: most points are won by the player who misses last; the crosscourt rally is the safest place to outlast. How: 10-ball tolerance rallies, margin over the net. Measure: UE rate, rally win %. Impact: ★★★★★

4 · Fitness & recovery positioning

Why: being in position turns "great gets" into easy balls and wins late sets. How: athlete development + bisector recovery. Measure: 3rd-set win %, balls reached in time. Impact: ★★★★☆

5 · Decision-making / shot selection

Why: attacking the right ball and changing direction only off inside balls eliminates the biggest error source. How: the Decision Engine + Home Base drill. Measure: directional-change errors, winner:UE. Impact: ★★★★★

6 · Second-serve reliability

Why: it sets your floor and stops free attacks. How: spin + depth, 90%+ make under pressure. Measure: double faults, 2nd-serve points won. Impact: ★★★★☆

Anti-distraction — the low-value time-sinks

⚠️ Stop over-investing here
  • The 210 km/h (130 mph) flat serve when your second serve and location are weak — chase % and placement first.
  • Trick shots (tweeners, behind-the-back) — near-zero match value.
  • Going for lines in practice "winners" — trains a habit that loses matches.
  • Obsessing over one ugly-looking stroke that rarely appears or rarely costs points (low Frequency in the Improvement Engine).
  • New rackets/strings every month instead of grooving patterns.
  • Out-rallying pushers on their terms instead of approaching the net.

Putting it to work

Audit your last month of practice: what % of time went to the six high-return skills above vs the distractions? Re-allocate. Combine this with the Improvement Engine — the high-return skill that's also your biggest weakness is your single best investment.

✅ Success metrics
  • ≥ 80% of practice time is on high-return skills.
  • The KPIs tied to skills 1–6 are all trending up.
  • You've consciously dropped at least one time-sink.
