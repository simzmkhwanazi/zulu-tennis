Practice Blueprint

Most practice is just "hitting." These plans turn court time into training — every session built around your top priority, ending with point play.

🎾 Courtside · Practice B
Every session: one priority + point play.
✓ Do — warm-up → your #1 priority → patterns → compete → cool-down.
✗ Don't — rally aimlessly with no priority and no pressure.
Always finish competing
🎾 Drill: 1-hour plan — 10 warm-up · 15 priority · 15 patterns · 15 point play.
🎯 Rule of every session

Warm up → technical/priority block → patterns → point play (always finish competing) → cool-down. Always include match-like pressure. Level B

Monthly improvement plan

  • Week 1–2: heaviest focus on the top priority (most reps, most pressure).
  • Week 3: integrate it into patterns & point play.
  • Week 4: test it in practice matches; re-measure the KPI.
  • End of month: re-score the Improvement Engine; set next month's focus.

Tournament-prep plan

The taper and sharpening details live in the Tournament OS countdown — in short: build > 14 days out, sharpen patterns at 7–14 days, taper the final week, keep it crisp.

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • "Practising" by rallying aimlessly with no priority and no point play.
  • Only drilling cooperatively — never under match pressure.
  • Working strengths because they're fun, ignoring the priority weakness.
✅ Success metrics
  • Every session names a priority and ends with point play.
  • Sessions logged (Practice Sessions) with a quality rating.
  • The monthly KPI for your priority improves.
