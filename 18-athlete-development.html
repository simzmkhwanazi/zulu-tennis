Athlete Development · off-court

Matches are won by the better athlete, not just the better ball-striker. This is the off-court engine — gym, speed, recovery, nutrition and the mind — that lets your tactics survive three sets.

🎾 Courtside · Athlete Development A
Train the athlete: power, speed, recover.
✓ Do — 2 gym + speed/agility sessions weekly; sleep 7–9 h; fuel before & after.
✗ Don't — do all tennis with no base, or skip prehab until it hurts.

Physical training

🏋️ Strength & power

Tennis is repeated explosive efforts. Train for force and power, not bodybuilding. 2× per week in-season.

🏋️ Speed, agility & footwork
  • Acceleration: 5–10 m sprints, wall drives — the first step wins balls.
  • Agility / change of direction: 5-10-5 shuttle, figure-8, reactive cone drills.
  • Footwork patterns: ladder, split-step timing, recovery cross-overs, shadow movement to court positions.
  • Multidirectional decel: braking drills — being able to stop = balanced contact.
🏋️ Endurance & energy systems

Tennis = repeated 5–15s efforts with short recovery, over hours. Train both: interval work (e.g. 15s on / 15s off) for the rally engine, and an aerobic base (steady 30–40 min) for recovery between points and matches.

🏋️ Mobility & flexibility

Daily 5–10 min: hips, thoracic spine (rotation), shoulders, ankles. Mobility buys you clean positions and protects joints under repeated load.

Injury prevention (prehab)

🩺 Protect the tennis joints
  • Shoulder/elbow/wrist: rotator-cuff & scapular work (band external rotations, Ys/Ts), grip/forearm work — the serve loads these hard.
  • Hips/knees/ankles: glute activation, single-leg balance, ankle stability — the brakes and the engine.
  • Warm-up (before): dynamic — leg swings, lunges, gradual hitting build-up. Never cold-serve.
  • Cool-down (after): easy movement + light stretching; start recovery immediately.

Recovery & lifestyle

🩺 Recover like it's training (because it is)
  • Sleep: the #1 recovery tool — aim 7–9 h; consistency matters most. Level A
  • Active recovery: easy movement, mobility, light spin the day after hard sessions.
  • Load management: don't stack max-intensity days; track soreness & readiness (see log below).

Hydration & nutrition

🩺 Fuel for performance
  • Daily: enough quality carbs (your fuel), protein for repair, plenty of water. Don't under-eat training.
  • Match day: a familiar carb-based meal 2–3 h prior; hydrate early (not just when thirsty).
  • During: water + electrolytes on changeovers; a banana/gel in long matches. Nothing new on match day.
  • After: carbs + protein within ~60 min to kick-start recovery.

Off-court mental training

  • Visualization: rehearse patterns and pressure points; see yourself executing your autopilot patterns.
  • Focus training: short daily breath/attention practice to strengthen the "reset" muscle from Mental Performance.
  • Confidence: keep a wins/progress log; review weapons before matches.
  • Journaling: a 2-line post-match note (what worked / one fix) feeds the Improvement Engine.

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • All tennis, no physical base — you lose the third set and the long rallies.
  • Skipping prehab until something hurts (shoulders/elbows especially).
  • Under-sleeping and under-fueling, then wondering why focus fades late.
  • Max intensity every day with no recovery — the fast track to injury and stagnation.
✅ Success metrics
  • Fitness-test numbers improving each re-test.
  • You're as sharp in the third set as the first (third-set win % ↑).
  • Fewer niggles/missed weeks; consistent sleep & load logged.
