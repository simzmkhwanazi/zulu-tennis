How the system works — the thinking behind it

Core philosophy

The objective is not to hit better shots — it is to win more matches and become a better athlete on and off the court. Every recommendation is weighed through five lenses:

Lens 1
Match Win %
Lens 2
Expected Value
Lens 3
Risk vs Reward
Lens 4
Repeatability
Lens 5
Long-Term Gain
Expected Value > highlight shots · Repeatability > perfect execution · Pressure reliability > practice level

The Decision Engine

Zulu Tennis runs like an in-match decision engine. The question is never “what's a nice shot?” but: given the score, situation, opponent, ball quality and my balance — what is the highest-value decision that increases my probability of winning the point? Every decision weighs ten inputs:

  1. Score Situation
  2. Point Importance
  3. Court Position
  4. Incoming Ball Quality
  5. Player Balance
  6. Opponent Position
  7. Opponent Weaknesses
  8. Risk Level
  9. Expected Reward
  10. Recovery Position
