AI Review Framework

Turn any AI assistant into your analyst. Paste your match data into these prompts and get structured, actionable feedback — then feed it back into your Improvement Engine.

🧠 How to use

Fill the [bracketed] parts with your real data (from charts, scouting, the data model), tap Copy, and paste into your AI tool. Keep the structure so answers stay consistent. Level D

1 · Match review

You are my tennis performance analyst. Here is my singles match report:
- Result/score: [score]
- Opponent type: [pusher / power baseliner / etc.]
- Stats: 1st serve % [x], 1st-serve pts won [x], 2nd-serve pts won [x],
  return pts won [x], winners [x], unforced errors [x], forced errors [x],
  break points won/faced [x/x], net pts won [x/x], avg rally length [x]
- What it felt like: [2-3 sentences]

Tasks:
1) Identify my THREE biggest tactical problems in this match.
2) For each, give the likely cause and one concrete fix.
3) Tell me the single highest-value thing to practise next and why.
Be specific and concise.

2 · Tactical analysis

Act as a tennis tactician. My game: [archetype + 2 weapons + 2 weaknesses].
Recurring pattern I lose with: [describe the rally/situation].
1) Explain the geometry/probability of why I'm losing this pattern.
2) Give me the higher-percentage decision rule (when to reset vs attack).
3) Turn it into a "Decision Card": situation, rule, why, wrong vs correct,
   a drill, and a success metric.

3 · Opponent scouting

Build me a game plan against this opponent:
- Type/hand: [type, R/L]
- Serve tendencies: [...]  Return tendencies: [...]
- FH/BH stability: [...]  Net skills: [...]
- Pressure/tiebreak/break-point behaviour: [...]
- Their winning patterns: [...]  Their losing patterns: [...]

Give me: (a) ONE strength to neutralise, (b) ONE weakness to attack,
(c) ONE big-point tendency to exploit, and (d) my 3-line match plan.

4 · Training planning

Create a 4-week training block for me.
- Level: [intermediate/advanced/tournament/elite amateur]
- Top priority weakness (Impact×Frequency×Ease ranked): [#1], then [#2]
- Days/week available: [x]  Court+gym access: [...]
Build a week-by-week plan (on-court priority blocks + off-court physical),
each session ending with point play, plus the KPI to track for each priority.

5 · Tournament review

Here are my results & stats from [event] across [n] matches: [paste].
1) What patterns held up under pressure and what broke down?
2) What's the ONE recurring issue across matches?
3) Give me a recovery + next-block plan for the 3 weeks after this event.

6 · Video analysis

From my video review grades (A-D): shot selection [x], positioning [x],
recovery [x], patterns [x], serve location [x], routine [x].
Plus notes: [paste any observations].
1) Which graded area should I fix first for the biggest win-% gain?
2) Give me 2 specific drills for it and a way to re-grade in 4 weeks.

7 · Weakness prioritization

Here are my weaknesses with Impact/Frequency/Ease scores (1-5):
[weakness — I/F/E], [weakness — I/F/E], [weakness — I/F/E] ...
Compute Priority = Impact × Frequency × Ease, rank them, tell me which ONE
to train now, which to ignore for now, and why — in terms of win probability.
⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Vague input ("I played bad") → vague output. Paste real numbers.
  • Acting on advice you never measure — always attach a KPI and a re-check date.
  • Treating AI output as gospel; sanity-check against this system and a coach.
✅ Success metrics
  • Each match/video produces one AI-assisted priority with a KPI.
  • Prompts are reused consistently, so feedback is comparable over time.
  • Priorities flow into the Improvement Engine and Roadmap.
