Match Intelligence Database

In a league or circuit you face the same opponents repeatedly. A scouting record turns "I always lose to her" into "she can't hurt me on the backhand and folds at 30–30." Build the file once, win with it for years.

Why scout

Most opponents are creatures of habit — a favourite serve on break point, a backhand that breaks down under pace, a tendency to rush when ahead. Capture those tendencies and you walk on court already two moves ahead. Each opponent is an Opponent Profile record. Level B

Turning notes into a plan

Before the match, distil the sheet into three lines for your Set-Break planner: one thing they do well (neutralise), one weakness (attack), one big-point tendency (exploit). Match the plan to their type via Player-Type Strategies.

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Scouting everything and remembering nothing — distil to 3 actionable points.
  • Never updating the sheet — players change; note what's different each time.
  • Letting their reputation psych you out instead of attacking their actual weaknesses.
✅ Success metrics
  • A scouting sheet exists for every repeat opponent.
  • Win rate vs repeat opponents improves over time.
  • You execute the "neutralise/attack/exploit" plan from the first game.
