Attacking System

Attacking isn't hitting harder — it's recognising the right ball, moving correctly, and finishing with the highest-percentage option. Most points are lost by attacking the wrong ball.

Recognising the attackable ball

Three cues, ideally two or more present at once: the ball is short (landing inside the baseline), sitting up (above net height at contact), and you can take it in balance. Miss these and you're forcing, not attacking.

The "Should I attack?" checklist

📋 Attack only if…
  • The ball is landing inside the baseline (short).
  • You can contact it at or above net height (sitting up).
  • You are balanced and moving forward, not stretched.
  • You have a target already in mind (open court / behind them).

If two or more are missing → keep constructing. Don't gift the point.

🌳 Attack decision tree
SHORT BALL ARRIVES —
   │
   ▼
Can I take it in balance, at/above net height?
   ├─ NO ─▶ Rally ball deep, recover, stay patient
   └─ YES ─▶ Approach or drive?
              ├─ Very short / low ─▶ APPROACH + close net
              └─ Sitting up high ──▶ DRIVE / swing volley, finish

Transition footwork

The error most amateurs make: hitting the approach while still running, then stopping. Do the opposite — load, hit, then keep moving forward and split-step as the opponent strikes.

  • Read short early → explode forward.
  • Hit through the ball moving forward (don't camp behind it).
  • Continue toward the net; split-step just before their contact.
  • Close the line you hit down.

Approach-shot selection

The short ball is…Approach choiceWhy
Very low / awkwardSlice deep down-the-lineStays low, buys you time to close
Sitting up, mid-courtDrive deep down-the-lineShortest path to net to defend; pressures them
Wide & shortDrive behind them / sharp angleThey're recovering the other way
🎯 The golden rule

Approach down the line by default — it's the shortest distance to the net, so you have time to get into position and you only have to cover one passing lane. Level A

Closing & finishing

  • First volley: deep and simple — you don't have to win it outright, just stay on offense.
  • Position: close tight after a good approach; hang back a touch after a weak one.
  • Finish: into the open court or behind a scrambling opponent — see Net Play.
⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Attacking a ball that's deep or below net height — the #1 unforced-error source.
  • Approaching crosscourt (longer path to net, two passing lanes to cover).
  • Over-hitting the approach for a winner instead of setting up the volley.
  • Floating to the net without a split-step — you're a sitting duck for the pass.
🎾 Practice drill — "Short-ball green light"
  • Setup: partner feeds a mix of deep and short balls randomly.
  • Reps: 5 × 2-min. Attack only the genuine short balls; rally the deep ones.
  • Constraint: attacking a deep ball = lose the point; you must close the net after every approach.
  • Coaching cue: "Read it, step in, hit through, split-step."
  • Success metric: > 60% of approached points won; near-zero attacks on deep balls.
✅ Success metrics
  • Net points won % climbing (track in Analytics).
  • Approach-shot success rate > 60%.
  • Errors on "attacks" of deep/low balls trending to zero.
