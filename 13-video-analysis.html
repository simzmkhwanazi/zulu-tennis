Video Analysis

Film hides nothing. The camera shows you the decisions and positioning you can't feel in the moment. Watch with a system, not just for fun — grade, don't spectate.

Film it right

  • Camera behind the baseline, elevated (fence-top / high tripod) — you want to see depth, position and the whole court.
  • Capture full points, not just rallies — the recovery and the space between points matter.
  • One match a week is plenty. Watch within 48 hours while memory is fresh.

What to track

Watch forThe question
Shot selectionDid I attack the right ball? Change direction off inside balls only?
Court positioningWas I behind the baseline by default? Stepping in on short balls?
RecoveryDid I get back to the bisector before their contact?
PatternsWhich patterns did I run? Did I have a plan or react?
Serve locationsWhere did serves actually land vs. where I intended?
Body language / routineDid I run my between-point routine, especially after errors?

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Only watching your winners — the errors and the decisions teach more.
  • Watching without a rubric, so it becomes entertainment, not analysis.
  • Filming from ground level at the side — you can't judge depth or position.
✅ Success metrics
  • One graded review per week, stored with the match.
  • Your lowest category grade climbs over a month.
  • Video confirms (or corrects) what the stats suggested.
