The Decision Engine · 01 / 07

Win the point before you hit it.

Most points aren’t lost on technique. They’re lost by attacking the wrong ball. One habit fixes that.

↓
02 / 07 · The idea

Picture a line down the middle.

A line between you and your opponent. Every ball is either outside that line, or inside it. That is the whole decision.

↓
03 / 07 · Outside the line

Outside? Reset.

If the ball pulls you wide, don’t try to change direction. Send it back crosscourt — high, with margin — and live to fight the next ball. That’s the rally in the video.

↓
04 / 07 · Inside the line

Inside, and balanced? Attack.

Now you have the green light. Change direction, take the open court, and finish the point on your terms.

↓
05 / 07 · In between

Then get back to the middle.

After every ball, recover to centre and wait. Stay patient. The inside ball will come.

↓
06 / 07 · Groove it

“Cross until inside.”

Rally only crosscourt. You may change direction just once — the moment you get a genuine inside ball. Count your unforced errors before and after; watch them fall.

↓
07 / 07 · Remember this

Reset the outside ball.
Attack the inside ball.
