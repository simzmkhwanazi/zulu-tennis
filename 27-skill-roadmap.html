Skill-Level Roadmap

The right priority depends on where you are. What an intermediate should obsess over is noise for an elite amateur. Find your level, train its priorities, hit its benchmarks, move up.

Intermediate

Primary focus: consistency & the Decision Engine basics — stop beating yourself.

Secondary: reliable second serve, deep returns, one go-to pattern.

Common mistakes: over-hitting, aiming the lines, no plan, attacking the wrong ball.

Metrics: UE count, 1st-serve %, return-in-play %. Match priority: rally tolerance + the Home Base rule.

Progress when: you can rally with margin, make 2nd serves, and run one pattern under mild pressure.

Advanced

Primary focus: patterns & weapons — develop a serve+1 and a baseline weapon.

Secondary: net finishing, defense/reset, score-based aggression.

Common mistakes: running pattern finishes too early; weak under pressure; one-dimensional.

Metrics: 1st-serve pts won, BP conversion, net pts won, winner:UE. Match priority: impose your 2 primary patterns.

Progress when: you reliably hold with serve+1, finish at net, and adapt aggression to the score.

Tournament

Primary focus: match intelligence & pressure — scouting, score-based play, closing out.

Secondary: physical durability over 3 sets, recovery between matches.

Common mistakes: poor tournament prep, choking on big points, no opponent plan.

Metrics: tie-break & deciding-set win %, BP save %, first-2-games win rate. Match priority: autopilot patterns + routine on big points.

Progress when: you win the matches you "should," close leads, and survive multi-match days.

Elite Amateur

Primary focus: marginal gains & weapon amplification — turn strengths into match-enders, eliminate the last exploitable weakness.

Secondary: surface/condition adaptation, advanced analytics & EV decisions.

Common mistakes: neglecting physical edge; predictable patterns vs strong scouting; plateauing without data.

Metrics: the full KPI dashboard vs elite benchmarks; win % vs each opponent type.

Progress when: your weapons hold up vs the best, you adapt to any surface, and your data trends elite.

Progression benchmarks

Cross-reference the analytics benchmark table for the numbers; you've "graduated" a level when you consistently hit the next column's KPIs in real matches, not just practice.

⚠️ Common mistakes
  • Training elite-level details while leaking intermediate-level unforced errors.
  • Skipping a level's fundamentals because they're "boring."
  • Judging your level by your best day, not your average match.
✅ Success metrics
  • You know your current level and its #1 priority.
  • Your level's key metrics are improving toward the next tier.
  • You're not skipping fundamentals to chase advanced toys.
