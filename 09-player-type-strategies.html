Player-Type Strategies

Pusher

Strengths: gets everything back, no free errors, infinite patience. Weaknesses: little pace of their own, dislikes net pressure & pace changes, struggles when pulled forward.

Game plan: don't out-push them and don't over-hit. Build with depth, draw the short ball, approach the net and finish. Use the drop shot–approach combo to drag them in.

Avoid: impatient over-hitting, getting into a "who blinks first" rally on their terms, beating yourself with errors.

Counter-Puncher

Strengths: elite defense, speed, redirects your pace, thrives on long rallies. Weaknesses: generating their own offense, being rushed, finishing at net.

Game plan: take time away with depth and forward pressure; serve+1 and approach; make them create. Bring them to net where they're least comfortable.

Avoid: long neutral rallies that play to their strength; going for too much and feeding them counters.

Power Baseliner

Strengths: big groundstrokes, dictates, takes time away. Weaknesses: being moved & rushed, low/short balls, having to generate their own pace off slow balls.

Game plan: first-strike (serve, return depth), change height & pace (slice, high heavy), move them side-to-side, take their time with depth-down-the-middle to kill angles. Use defense to absorb and reset.

Avoid: trading pace toe-to-toe from the baseline; standing too far back and becoming a target.

Serve-and-Volleyer

Strengths: forward pressure, quick points, strong volleys/serve. Weaknesses: low returns at their feet, lobs, passing lanes when rushed.

Game plan: return low & at the feet (chip/dip), mix the lob, take pace off to make them volley up. On their net rush, aim through the middle to cut their angle.

Avoid: floating returns up; trying to pass with a low-percentage rip when a dip-and-lob does the job.

All-Court Player

Strengths: variety, adapts, no glaring hole. Weaknesses: can be out-committed by a clearer, more disciplined plan; pick their relative weaker wing.

Game plan: impose your identity, don't get drawn into a variety contest. Find the marginally weaker side and pressure it repeatedly with your best pattern.

Avoid: matching their variety and losing your own plan; indecision.

Moonballer

Strengths: high heavy balls push you back, disrupt rhythm, draw errors. Weaknesses: low/fast balls, net pressure, being made to hit down.

Game plan: take the high ball early/on the rise or move back and reset, then approach; use the swing volley; slice low to change the ball they like. See dealing with high heavy topspin.

Avoid: letting every ball bounce above your shoulder and grinding on their terms; impatience.

Left-Hander

Strengths: wide ad-court slice serve into your backhand, forehand cross to your backhand, reversed angles. Weaknesses: often the backhand; the same lefty patterns become predictable.

Game plan: serve/return to their backhand (usually the deuce-court wide), prepare for the ad-court kick serve, and remember crosscourt now feeds their forehand — favour deep middle. Practise against lefties deliberately.

Avoid: forgetting the spin direction on big points; feeding their forehand crosscourt repeatedly.

Big Server

Strengths: free points on serve, cheap holds, big first strike. Weaknesses: return games, second serves, sometimes movement & backhand.

Game plan: stand back, block returns deep through the middle, win the second-serve points, and hold easily yourself so every game pressures their serve. Treat every return game as a free swing — relax and make them play.

Avoid: pressing for return winners; losing focus in your own service games and letting it go to a breaker.

Junkballer

Strengths: constant variety — slices, spins, changes of pace, awkward heights. Weaknesses: little raw pace; predictable once you accept the rhythm-disruption.

Game plan: generate your own pace (don't rely on theirs), move in on the short junk and attack, stay patient and pick the ball you can drive. Keep your own depth so their variety has no time.

Avoid: getting frustrated by the lack of rhythm; trying to crush every odd ball.

⚠️ Universal mistake

Playing the same way against everyone. The fix: name the opponent type in the warm-up, pick the one strength to neutralise and one weakness to attack, and commit to that plan for the first few games.

✅ Success metrics
  • You can state your plan (neutralise X, attack Y) before the first point.
  • Win rate by opponent type tracked over a season (in Analytics / Scouting DB).
  • Fewer "I had no plan" losses to awkward styles (pushers, junkballers).
